# Urban Heat Island (UHI)

The phenomenon where urban areas are measurably warmer than surrounding rural areas, caused by altered land surfaces, reduced vegetation, waste heat from human activity, and urban morphology that traps radiation.

## Key Facts

- UHI is the central object of study in this wiki — all other concepts connect back to it.
- Two sub-types: **Surface UHI** (land surface temperature, measured by remote sensing) and **Canopy UHI** (air temperature at human height, measured by weather stations).
- Intensity is expressed as the temperature difference (°C) between urban core and rural surroundings.
- Cities like [[wiki/entities/muenchen]] and [[wiki/entities/nuernberg]] are at elevated UHI risk due to high [[wiki/concepts/impervious-surface]] coverage.

## Mechanisms / How It Works

1. **Impervious surfaces** absorb and retain solar radiation instead of reflecting or evaporating it → see [[wiki/concepts/impervious-surface]]
2. **Reduced vegetation** eliminates [[wiki/concepts/evapotranspiration]] cooling
3. **Urban canyon effect** — buildings trap longwave radiation emitted from surfaces
4. **Waste heat** from vehicles, HVAC systems, industry
5. **Altered wind patterns** reduce convective cooling

## Debates and Uncertainties

- How to best measure UHI: satellite LST vs. ground-based air temperature networks give different readings.
- Whether nocturnal or daytime UHI intensity matters more for health outcomes.
