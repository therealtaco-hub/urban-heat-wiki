# Impervious Surface / Surface Sealing (Versiegelung)

Sealed, non-permeable urban surfaces — roads, parking lots, rooftops, paved plazas — that replace natural, vegetated soil. The primary land-use driver of [[wiki/concepts/urban-heat-island]] intensity.

## Key Facts

- Impervious surfaces absorb solar radiation and re-emit it as heat; natural soils and vegetation instead use that energy for evapotranspiration.
- [[wiki/entities/muenchen]] and [[wiki/entities/nuernberg]] are among Germany's **most heavily sealed cities**. *(Source: [[wiki/sources/gegen-hitze-kuehlleistung-baum]])*
- Each parking space occupies **12–20 m²** of potentially green space. *(Source: [[wiki/sources/gegen-hitze-kuehlleistung-baum]])*
- Bavaria's *Modernisierungsgesetz* (2025) reportedly removes municipalities' power to **prohibit new surface sealing** — a significant policy regression. *(Source: [[wiki/sources/gegen-hitze-kuehlleistung-baum]])*

## Mechanisms / How It Works

- Sealed surfaces have high heat capacity and low albedo → absorb and store daytime heat.
- No vegetation → no [[wiki/concepts/evapotranspiration]] → no latent heat flux to cool the surface.
- Nocturnal re-emission of stored heat prolongs elevated temperatures overnight.
- Underground parking structures (Tiefgaragen) are a compounding problem: they seal the surface *and* prevent tree root depth, eliminating any future greening potential. *(Source: [[wiki/sources/gegen-hitze-kuehlleistung-baum]])*
