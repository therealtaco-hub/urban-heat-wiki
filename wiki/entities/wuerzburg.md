# Würzburg

**The primary target city of this wiki and the user's research project.** A Franconian city in Bavaria, Germany, with a documented heat problem and the subject of ongoing remote sensing research by the University of Würzburg and DLR.

## Overview

Würzburg lies in a geographically protected valley (the Main river valley) and is known for high summer temperatures. The University of Würzburg's Institute of Geography and Geology is an active producer of urban heat research — most notably the García de León et al. study on LST and tree cover in Munich, which is methodologically identical to what the user's project aims to do for Würzburg.

## Project Goals for Würzburg

1. **LST–tree density correlation**: map LST (from Landsat/aerial) against tree cover density across the city; demonstrate cooling in high-canopy areas vs. bare asphalt/concrete.
2. **Vulnerability mapping**: integrate Zensus 2022 data to identify grid cells with high concentrations of elderly (Rentner) and young children (Kleinkinder) — prioritise greening interventions in these areas.
3. **Simulation**: quantify expected temperature reduction from planting X trees at location Y, and the effect of unsealing area Z (temperature, infiltration, groundwater).

## Key Data Needed

- Würzburg-specific LST data (Landsat scenes, summer months, multi-year)
- Tree inventory data (city GIS, ideally individual crown data)
- Land use map (ATKIS or city GIS)
- Zensus 2022 demographic grid (100m resolution)
- Building footprints and heights (for LCZ/morphology analysis)
