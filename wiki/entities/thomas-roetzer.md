# Thomas Rötzer

Forest scientist (*Forstwissenschaftler*) at [[wiki/entities/tu-muenchen]], known for developing a methodology to precisely quantify the cooling output of individual urban trees.

## Overview

Rötzer's work focuses on measuring tree-level cooling contributions in urban environments, combining physical measurements (height, crown diameter, leaf density, shadow cast) into a single cooling capacity figure in kWh/year.

## Role in Urban Heat Research

- Developed the tree cooling quantification methodology (height, crown diameter, leaf density, shadow cast → kWh/year cooling).
- Served as dissertation committee **chair** for Stratopoulos-Le Chalony's "Klimabäume" study on drought vs. cooling tradeoffs. *(Source: [[wiki/sources/klimabaeume-fuer-die-stadt]])*
- **Co-author** on García de León et al. — the Munich LST+tree canopy study from Würzburg/DLR. *(Source: [[wiki/sources/garcia-de-leon-lst-trees-munich]])*
- Now at School of Life Sciences, TU München (Freising campus).
