# University of Würzburg — Institute of Geography and Geology

The primary academic institution producing remote sensing-based urban heat research in the Würzburg context. Multiple members of the institute are co-authors on the García de León et al. Munich LST+trees study — the paper most directly relevant to the user's Würzburg project.

## Overview

The institute works at the intersection of remote sensing, GIS, and urban climate. It has a close partnership with the German Aerospace Center (DLR) — Hannes Taubenböck holds a joint appointment between DLR and the University of Würzburg, and Tobias Leichtle is at DLR's German Remote Sensing Data Center in Weßling.

## Key Researchers (from García de León et al.)

- **Andrea Sofía García de León** — lead author, LST and trees
- **Tobias Ullmann** — remote sensing
- **Antonio Castañeda-Gómez** — remote sensing
- **Nils Karges** — remote sensing
- **Hannes Taubenböck** — joint DLR/Univ. Würzburg, senior researcher in urban remote sensing

## Role in Urban Heat Research

Produced the direct methodological template for the user's project: correlating downscaled LST with individual tree crown data across Munich's land use classes. The same team would be the natural academic partner or reference for a Würzburg replication.
