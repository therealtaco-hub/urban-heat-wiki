# München (Munich)

Major Bavarian city and primary German case study for urban heat and green infrastructure loss, notable for high impervious surface coverage and accelerating tree loss.

## Overview

Munich is one of Germany's largest cities and is identified — alongside [[wiki/entities/nuernberg]] — as among the **most heavily sealed (impervious surface) cities in Germany**. Despite awareness of the urban heat problem, the city is currently losing more trees than it gains.

## Role in Urban Heat Research

- High sealing + high tree loss = compounding UHI risk.
- **Moosach district**: planned construction of ~800 new apartments with underground parking — cited as exemplary case of densification destroying tree-planting potential.
- City financially subsidises greened courtyards (*begrünte Innenhöfe*) but uptake is described as rare.
- Institutional actor: *Referat für Klima- und Umweltschutz* (Dept. for Climate and Environmental Protection) — contact: Cornelia Leupold.
- Advocacy actor: *Bund Naturschutz* — source of the 2,000 trees/year loss figure.

## Policy Context

- Bavaria's *Modernisierungsgesetz* (2025): removes municipal power to prohibit surface sealing — researchers fear this will worsen Munich's trajectory. *(Source: [[wiki/sources/gegen-hitze-kuehlleistung-baum]])*
